## 1. Project direction

**Research question (working version):**

> Does the reconfiguration of functional connectivity from low to high working-memory load (0-back → 2-back) **predict** individual working-memory performance?

**Dataset:** HCP N-back **with behaviour** (`load_hcp_task_with_behaviour`) — 100 subjects, 360 ROIs (Glasser parcellation), 12 networks (Cole-Anticevic). Networks of interest: **Frontoparietal, Default, Cingulo-Opercular** (= salience in this atlas). *PTN is not allowed.*

## 2. Pipeline — agreed (6 steps)

1. **Data ingestion** — load BOLD time series per subject (WM task).
2. **Condition segmentation** — use the EV files to split timepoints into 0-back vs 2-back.
3. **Functional connectivity** — Pearson correlation per condition → **2 matrices per subject** (0-back, 2-back).
4. **Graphs** — convert each matrix into a graph (nodes = ROIs, edges = connections).
5. **Graph metrics** — segregation / integration (e.g. modularity, global/local efficiency, participation coefficient).
6. **Testing / prediction** — test whether the graph measures (or the 0→2-back change) **predict working-memory performance**.
