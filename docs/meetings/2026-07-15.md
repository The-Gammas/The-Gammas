## Hypothesis (Arefeh's, refined)

> Increasing WM load from 0-back to 2-back reconfigures whole-brain FC from a more **segregated** toward a more **integrated** state; and individual differences in this reconfiguration **predict** 2-back performance (`acc_2bk`, or **d′**) in **held-out subjects** — those who reconfigure more toward integration tend to perform better.

> **Directionality** — the *primary* test stays **non-directional** (does reconfiguration predict performance at all, against a permutation null). *"Toward integration → better"* is the expectation we test, **not an assumption**: prior work (Cohen & D'Esposito; Murphy et al.) shows integration is *selective*, not uniformly adaptive, so a negative-direction result is still valid. *(Per our [research-proposal](../../manuscript/research-proposal.md).)*
